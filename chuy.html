Chui region

Chui Oblast is located in the Chui Valley in the north of Kyrgyzstan. It was created as Frunze
Oblast on November 21, 1939 by the Decree of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the
USSR, and dissolved in 1959. In 1990, it was restored under the current name from districts
under republican control. It borders the Republic of Kazakhstan to the north and west, Talas and Jalal-Abad to the
southwest, Naryn to the south, and Issyk-Kul regions of Kyrgyzstan to the southeast .

Burana Tower

Burana is a tower belonging to the IX-XI centuries of the Karakhanid
state located in the Chui Valley in the north of Kyrgyzstan.
The tower is part of the specially protected archeological-architectural complex "Burana". Also, the state
archeological-architectural building became a part of the museum reserve and was protected by the state as a
monument of cultural and historical heritage.
