Osh region

Osh region is the largest administrative-territorial unit in Kyrgyzstan . It is located in the south of the
Kyrgyz Republic. Jalal-Abad is bordered by Suzak district in the north , Uzbekistan in the northwest ,
Batken region in the west , Tajikistan in the south, Naryn region in the northeast and east , and China .
Osh region in 1939. It was organized on November 21, 1959. On January 27, 1990, it merged with

The Sulayman Mountain

Sulaiman-Too is a holy mountain in Osh , Kyrgyzstan . In June 2009, it was the
first monument in the country to be included in the World Heritage List [1] . Mount Sulaiman is located in the center of Osh, on
the left bank of Ak-Buura river . It is 3 km long, 1.5-2 km wide, and 1176 m above sea level. The relative
height is 300-350 m. Sulaiman-Too is a national historical and archeological museum-complex. Some
people compare the mountain to a sinking mountain. The number of worshipers is 16-17 thousand
people a year. It is known that until the 16th century Sulaiman-Too was called Baara-Kuh. Sulaiman-Too
was the first state monument registered to UNESCO in June 2009.
